📊 Methodology note

Population data: 2024 estimates (Eurostat). Ecommerce penetration figures: approximate % of adults making online purchases regularly, drawn from Eurostat Digital Economy reports and industry surveys (2023–24). Sessions per million provides a normalised reach index — a high figure indicates strong organic or paid visibility relative to market size. Conversion benchmarks: EU ecommerce average sits around 2–3%; B2B-oriented stores with returning customers typically exceed this. Countries with under 100 sessions should be treated as statistically unreliable for conversion rate analysis. Small-volume countries (Croatia, Slovenia, Baltics, Malta, Cyprus, Luxembourg) have meaningful population-normalised sessions in some cases but insufficient volume to draw firm conversion conclusions.

Strategic conclusions & recommendations

PPC, SEO and market prioritisation — based on sessions, conversion, AOV, GP and ad efficiency

How to read this: The financial data reveals that conversion rate alone is a misleading performance indicator. Germany converts poorly but delivers €63.60 GP per order — the highest of any market. Ireland converts well and delivers €43.90 GP per order at only €6.30 ad spend per order — the most efficient market in the portfolio. GB (Envelopes.co.uk) converts reasonably but returns only €22.10 GP per order while spending €12.90 per order on ads — the least efficient major market. The strategic priorities below are based on GP per order and ad efficiency, not conversion rate.
Tier 1 — Scale up immediately

Ireland — €43.90 GP/order, €6.30 ad/order. Your most efficient market by a significant margin. Only 199 orders and 2,819 sessions from a population of 5.1M with 91% ecommerce penetration. This market is being dramatically underinvested. Doubling PPC budget here would likely yield the best return of any spend in the portfolio. SEO investment in Irish-specific keywords would compound this further.

Germany — €63.60 GP/order, the highest in the portfolio. Reframe Germany as a premium acquisition market: accept a higher cost-per-click and higher ad spend per order because each order is worth nearly three times a GB order. Target high-intent, transactional keywords rather than broad packaging terms. Consider RLSA (remarketing lists for search) to recapture the comparison shoppers who browse without converting. Payment method investment (Rechnung/SEPA) is the single highest-leverage localisation fix.

Spain — €40.60 GP/order, €7.90 ad/order, 596 orders. Home market performing well. Maintain and grow — particularly through account-based retention of existing B2B customers rather than pure acquisition spend.

Tier 2 — Optimise and grow

France — €35.00 GP/order, 765 orders, highest volume. Strong but GP/order is lower than Germany, Ireland or Spain. The priority here is protecting GP margin rather than growing volume — understand why GP/order is lower than Spain despite similar conversion rates. Review product mix and discount levels in France.

Italy — €45.50 GP/order (second only to Germany) but €12.50 ad/order — high acquisition cost eating into an otherwise excellent market. Reducing ad spend per order through better keyword targeting or improving organic visibility would make Italy considerably more profitable. The conversion rate issue is partly structural (Italian B2B buying behaviour) but the GP/order when you do convert is very strong.

Portugal — €28.30 GP/order, only €7.20 ad/order. Quietly efficient. 157 orders suggests it is underinvested relative to its efficiency. Worth modest budget increase and SEO attention.

Tier 3 — Fix before scaling

GB Envelopes.co.uk — The most important finding in this dataset. €22.10 GP/order with €12.90 ad/order is the worst ad efficiency of any major market. You are spending more on ads per order in GB than anywhere else, to acquire orders worth less than anywhere else. The strategic answer is not more PPC — it is SEO. France generates 765 orders at €9.30 ad/order; GB generates 754 orders at €12.90. The session gap (13,547 FR vs 7,728 GB) is the entire explanation. Closing that gap through organic search would transform the GB GP position without increasing ad spend.

Netherlands — Good conversion (6.85%) but only €24.50 GP/order and €10.60 ad/order. High delivery costs (€3,308 on €18k revenue) are compressing margin. Review delivery cost structure for NL.

Belgium & Czechia — Both showing €11+ ad/order against modest GP/order. Keyword targeting review recommended — likely broad match terms driving unqualified traffic.

PPC & SEO priorities summary

Increase PPC budget: Ireland (highest priority), Germany (accept higher CPC for premium orders), Portugal (efficient, underinvested)

Reduce/reallocate PPC: GB Envelopes (replace with SEO investment), Belgium, Czechia (tighten keyword match types)

SEO priorities: GB Envelopes (biggest single opportunity — closing the session gap with France would add ~€10k+ GP/month at current rates), Romania (19M population, near-zero visibility), Greece (10M population, invisible), Germany (organic rankings reduce reliance on high-CPC paid terms)

Localisation fixes that will move the needle: German payment methods (Rechnung/SEPA), Dutch iDEAL, Danish MobilePay, Norwegian Vipps, Polish PLN pricing display

Watch closely: Norway (new launch — benchmark now and track monthly), Italy (strong GP/order but high ad cost — optimisation opportunity)
